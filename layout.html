Physical Layout

Angkor Wat covers an area of 402 acres making it one of the largest religious monuments in the world. The central temple complex is the primary monument, consisting of five towers with one on each corner and one in the center. Measuring 4,920 feet long and 1,300 feet wide, the temple is decorated with extensive carvings and sculptures from Hindu mythology and the epics of Ramayana and Mahabharata. The temple is enclosed by a series of walls while the entire site is surrounded by a large moat. Angkor Wat itself is mainly a religious complex, with no residential structures. However, the site includes a large network of canals and reservoirs that were important for the water management system of the empire. Infrastructure is also present, connecting the temple to the surrounding larger city. There are no tombs in Angkor Wat, as it did not serve as a burial site.
