Time Period(s) of Major Occupation

Angkor Wat was constructed in the early 12th century by King Suryavarman II. The site became a focal point of the empire with the construction of Angkor Wat itself indicating major occupation of the site. After Khmer occupation, the site was reanimated as a Buddhist site in the 16th century. After the 19th century, the French Protectorate and post-independence Cambodia deeply transformed the site.
