Importance of the Site

Angkor Wat is prevalent because of its construction and cultural role in the dominant Khmer empire. The site is the largest religious monument and demonstrates advanced engineering, providing a gateway to understanding key points in Khmer history. Firstly, the construction showcases a level of development important in understanding the scale and sophistication of the Khmer Empire. Steep staircases and the use of different joints play a role in the site's longevity, but also prove an understanding of load distribution and aesthetics. The sandstone blocks used in the construction of the temple were transported 50 kilometers from the Kulen Mountains, not possible without a good foundation in logistics. The integration of this temple to a broader hydraulic network further communicates the scale of the Khmer Empire. These findings provide a new perspective of urban planning and demonstrate a level of development previously underestimated in premodern Southeast Asia. Secondly, the cultural and religious bas-reliefs depict scenes from Hindu epics such as the Ramayana and Mahabharata, alongside historical battles.
